// the thesis

The next era of work doesn't replace people.
It makes more businesses possible.

For most of history a real business needed real people. Then it needed software. Then it needed AI.

The next era needs a workforce — of humans, AI, and robots — sharing the same goals, the same governance, and the same evidence trail.

// worker autonomy levels · L0 → L7

Workers don't suddenly act on their own.
They earn it, level by level.

Continuous defines eight autonomy levels — from advisory observation to autonomous execution — and three trust tiers across them: advisory, cooperative, autonomous. You decide where each worker operates and what crosses the human approval line.

advisory · L0 – L2 cooperative · L3 – L5 autonomous · L6 – L7
L0observes

Observe

Read scoped data and produce summaries.

e.g. read the inbox, summarize new leads
L1drafts

Draft

Draft responses, quotes, tasks, invoices, recommendations.

e.g. draft a $1,840 quote for review
L2internal

Internal action

Create internal tasks, update internal records, prepare artifacts.

e.g. open follow-up tasks, log activity
L3external

External comm

Send approved-template emails/SMS and update external systems under policy.

e.g. send the approved quote to the customer
L4prepares

Transaction prep

Prepare payments, filings, contracts, refunds, payroll — but never execute.

e.g. assemble a payroll run for approval
L5approved

Approved execution

Execute specific external actions after a human approval crosses the line.

e.g. charge a deposit on owner approval
L6bounded

Autonomous

Execute low-risk bounded actions without case-by-case approval.

e.g. send a reminder on day 7 of an unpaid invoice
L7expert

Managed expert

Operate with a human specialist, accountant, legal, or operator review network behind every move.

e.g. file a 941 with managed CPA review
